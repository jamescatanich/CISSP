
# CISSP Manager Mindset Guide

This folder contains a collection of study guides organized according to the eight domains of the Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) Common Body of Knowledge (CBK).

Unlike traditional certification notes that focus primarily on memorization, these guides emphasize the "manager mindset" expected on the CISSP exam. Each domain highlights key concepts, common exam traps, and the decision-making approach that ISC2 expects candidates to apply when evaluating security, risk, governance, and business objectives.

- `CISSP_Manager_Mindset_Guide_Domain_1.pdf` — Security and Risk Management
- `CISSP_Manager_Mindset_Guide_Domain_2.pdf` — Asset Security
- `CISSP_Manager_Mindset_Guide_Domain_3.pdf` — Security Architecture and Engineering
- `CISSP_Manager_Mindset_Guide_Domain_4.pdf` — Communication and Network Security

- `CISSP_Manager_Mindset_Guide_Domain_8.pdf` — Software Development Security

## Purpose

The primary goals of these guides are to:

- Reinforce core CISSP concepts and terminology.
- Develop the managerial and risk-based perspective required by ISC2.
- Highlight common exam pitfalls and misleading answer choices.
- Connect technical topics to business, governance, and organizational objectives.
- Serve as a rapid review resource before practice exams and the CISSP examination.

## Study Approach

These guides are intended to complement, not replace, official study materials such as:

- *ISC2 Official Study Guide (OSG) Tenth Editoin* - Helpful but authors are all over the place which makes it tougher than it needs to be to follow.
- *ISC2 Common Body of Knowledge (CBK) Sixth Edition* - Dry read but much easier to follow than the OSG.
- Quantum Exams - Practice questions and Computer Adaptive Tests (CATs) - Spend the money for the CAT.
- Supplemental video training and reference materials. Hit or miss videos online.

For best results:

1. Review the relevant guide before studying a domain in the OSG or CBK.
2. Focus on understanding *why* management decisions are made.
3. Pay special attention to the "exam traps" sections.
4. Practice selecting answers that prioritize risk management, governance, and business objectives over technical implementation.
